Steps

A1 Hola + Buenos días ~2 min

Listen to each phrase, then repeat it 3 times out loud. Focus on matching the rhythm — not just the words.

Hola Buenos días
Practice: Greet Diana as if it's 9 in the morning.
A2 Buenas tardes + Buenas noches ~2 min

When does each one start? Tardes around 12:00, noches around 20:00. Listen and repeat.

Buenas tardes Buenas noches
á Stress the accented syllable: dí-as, tar-des, no-ches — never flatten them. Días is two syllables (dee-as), not one.
≈ Cognate: nocturnal → noches — both share the Latin root for night.
Practice: Greet Diana using whichever greeting fits the current time right now.
A3 Quick review ~2 min

Diana says a time — you say the right greeting. 4 rounds, no peeking.

7:00 → ? 14:00 → ? 21:00 → ? any time → ?
🌎 Cultural note: in much of Latin America buenos días ends right at noon — switch to tardes from roughly 12:00–13:00.
