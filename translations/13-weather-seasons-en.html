Full Phrase Reference — Spanish → English
¿Qué tiempo hace?
What is the weather like?
The essential question. Stress: TIEM-po. Never confuse with ¿Qué hora es? (What time is it?)
¿Qué tiempo hace en Madrid?
What is the weather like in Madrid?
Add any city to extend the question. Opens natural small-talk.
Hace buen tiempo
The weather is nice / It is fine
Catch-all for pleasant weather. Opposite: hace mal tiempo.
Hace mal tiempo
The weather is bad
Useful catch-all — no need to specify exactly what is wrong.
Hace veinte grados
It is twenty degrees (Celsius)
Always Celsius in Spanish-speaking world. "Hace menos diez" = -10°C.
Hay tormenta
There is a storm
Hay (there is/are) + noun. Pattern: hay niebla, hay tormenta, hay hielo.
Hoy llueve. Mañana hace sol.
Today it is raining. Tomorrow it will be sunny.
Spanish uses present tense for near-future forecasts — no "will" equivalent needed.
En verano hace calor.
In summer it is hot.
En + season pattern. No article before the season in this construction.
Está despejado
It is clear / cloudless
Forecast register. Uses estar — sky state is temporary. Root: despejar (to clear out).
Hay chubascos
There are showers
Formal forecast term. In conversation you'd say "llueve." Know both registers.
¿Qué temperatura hace?
What is the temperature?
Stress: tem-pe-ra-TU-ra. Default stress rule: penultimate syllable.
Está parcialmente nublado
It is partly cloudy
5-syllable word: par-cial-MEN-te. Clap each syllable to lock the stress.
Tips & Tricks
🎯
The Hacer Rule
Spanish treats weather as something the sky does. Hace = "it makes/does." Never say es calor — that would mean "it is heat" (a thing, not a condition).
🌍
Hemisphere Flip
Buenos Aires, Santiago, and Lima have inverted seasons. December = summer, June = winter. Always ask which hemisphere when talking weather with Latin Americans.
🔊
Pronunciation
Frío = 2 syllables (FRI-o). Llueve = Latin America: yoo-EH-veh. Otoño — ñ sounds like "ny" in canyon: oh-TOH-nyo.
