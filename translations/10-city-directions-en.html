City Map — Annotated Vocabulary
Asking
Perdón, ¿dónde está el banco?
Excuse me, where is the bank?
Stress DON-de. Both syllables short — the accent marks the question word only.
Polite form
¿Me puede decir…?
Can you tell me…?
Formal upgrade of ¿dónde está? — infinitive decir never changes here.
Direction
Todo recto
Straight ahead
Recto = rectangle → goes forward. Don't confuse with derecha (right).
Direction
A la izquierda
To the left
Spelling: iz-QUIER-da. qu = /k/ not /kw/. Hold up your left hand when you say it.
Direction
A la derecha
To the right
Derecha → "the right" — both start with D. Hold up your right hand.
Position
Al lado del banco
Next to the bank
del = de + el (never "de el"). Feminine: al lado de la farmacia — no contraction.
Distance
Está cerca / lejos
It's near / far
cerca and lejos never change for gender — works for any noun.
Verb · formal
Gire / Siga / Cruce
Turn / Continue / Cross (polite)
Informal: gira, sigue, cruza. Use formal (–e) with strangers.
Ordinal
La primera calle
The first street
primera = feminine because calle is feminine. La segunda calle = the second street.
Sequence
Después del banco
After the bank
Landmark first, then action — "después del banco, gira" is the natural Spanish order.
Endpoint
Al final de la calle
At the end of the street
No contraction: de la (not del) because calle is feminine.
Time distance
A cinco minutos
Five minutes away
Spaniards give distance in time — a cinco minutos is natural; "5 km" sounds odd in conversation.

Street Dialogue — Asking
A: Perdón, ¿dónde está el banco?
Excuse me, where is the bank?
B: Todo recto, al final de la calle.
Straight ahead, at the end of the street.
A: Muchas gracias.
Thank you very much.
B: De nada.
You're welcome.
Street Dialogue — Polite Request
A: Perdón, ¿me puede decir dónde está la farmacia?
Excuse me, can you tell me where the pharmacy is?
B: Sí, claro. Gire a la izquierda y es la segunda calle.
Yes, of course. Turn left and it's the second street.
A: ¿Está lejos?
Is it far?
B: No, está muy cerca, a cinco minutos.
No, it's very close, five minutes away.
Common
Traps ⚠
a el banco
al banco
a + el always contracts to al
de el parque
del parque
de + el always contracts to del
recto (only)
todo recto
recto alone is accepted but todo recto is preferred
gira (to stranger)
gire (to stranger)
Use formal imperative with people you don't know
el farmacia
la farmacia
farmacia, estación, iglesia → la (feminine)
