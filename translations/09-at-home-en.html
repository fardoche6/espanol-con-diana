Rooms · Furniture · Location · Description

English Reference
House Floorplan — Rooms & Phrases
Una casa típica / A typical house
Location
¿Dónde está el baño?
Where is the bathroom?
Stress: DÓN-de — the accent matters!
Answer
Está al fondo a la derecha.
It's at the back on the right.
Real-life native answer — no full sentence needed.
Existence
Hay un jardín grande.
There is a large garden.
Hay = existence. Never "Hay el jardín" — no definite article after hay.
Description
El salón es grande pero el dormitorio es pequeño.
The living room is large but the bedroom is small.
pero = but · también = also — your two key connectors.
Preposition
La cama está al lado de la ventana.
The bed is next to the window.
de + el → del (contraction, always). Never "de el".
Key Phrases — 12 Focal Points
🏠
Home Type
Vivo en un piso.
I live in a flat.
piso = flat in Spain; apartamento in Latin America. Cognate anchor!
🛋️
Furniture
El sofá está en el salón.
The sofa is in the living room.
está = specific location (ser vs estar rule). El sofá is masc. despite -a ending.
🛏️
Bedroom
Duermo en el dormitorio.
I sleep in the bedroom.
dormir → duermo (o→ue stem change). Dormitory = same Latin root.
📍
On top of
El libro está encima de la mesa.
The book is on the table.
encima de = specifically on the surface. en = vaguer "in/at".
📦
Under
El gato está debajo de la cama.
The cat is under the bed.
debajo → think "de-base-of". Stress: de-BA-jo.
↔️
Next to
El sillón está al lado del sofá.
The armchair is next to the sofa.
al lado de + el = al lado DEL. Contraction is mandatory.
🚿
Shower
Me ducho en el baño.
I shower in the bathroom.
ducharse is reflexive — me/te/se. Don't drop the pronoun!
🪟
Between
La cama está entre la ventana y el armario.
The bed is between the window and the wardrobe.
entre + two nouns. armario = armoire (French cognate).
🏢
Floor
El baño está en el primer piso.
The bathroom is on the first floor.
Primer piso in Spain = 2nd floor in N. America. Ground = planta baja.
📚
Bookshelf
Hay una estantería en mi cuarto.
There is a bookshelf in my room.
es-tan-te-RÍ-a — 5 syllables, stress on the 4th. Tap it out!
🏡
Describe
Mi casa tiene tres habitaciones.
My house has three rooms.
Mi casa tiene… = starter phrase using tener (to have).
🪑
Plural
Las sillas están en la cocina.
The chairs are in the kitchen.
están = plural of está. Verb agrees with subject (las sillas).
Está vs Hay
The most common A1 error — master it here.
WRONG
Hay el sofá en el salón.
× Hay + definite article = always wrong
RIGHT
El sofá está en el salón.
✓ Specific thing → está (located)
RIGHT
Hay un sofá en el salón.
✓ Existence, indefinite → hay
The Golden Rule
If you hear yourself saying Hay el… or Hay la… — stop.

Hay = "there exists something" (unknown/indefinite)
Hay un baño en este piso. There's a bathroom on this floor.

Está = "this specific thing is somewhere" (located)
El baño está arriba. The bathroom is upstairs.

Test: can you point to it? Use está. Just saying it exists? Use hay.
Prepositions of Place
en
→
in / at (general)
El libro está en la habitación.
Vaguer than encima de — in/at the room, not on a specific surface.
encima de
→
on top of
El café está encima de la mesa.
Physically resting on a surface. More specific than en.
debajo de
→
under / below
El gato está debajo de la cama.
Think "de-BASE-of" → at the base. Stress: de-BA-jo.
al lado de
→
next to
El baño está al lado de la cocina.
de + el → del. "Al lado del sofá" — never "de el".
entre
→
between
El pasillo está entre la cocina y el salón.
Always takes two objects joined by y (and).
detrás de / delante de
→
behind / in front of
El escritorio está delante de la ventana.
detrás → "beyond-tras". Pair and drill together.
