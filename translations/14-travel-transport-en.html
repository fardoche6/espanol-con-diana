Block G · Buying a Ticket
Un billete de ida y vuelta para Sevilla.
A round-trip ticket to Seville.
Tip Ida = one-way (→). Ida y vuelta = round-trip (↔). Picture the arrows and you'll never mix them up.
Block C · Travel Verbs
El tren sale a las nueve.
The train leaves at nine.
Tip Sale = it leaves. "The train has a SALE on departures." Silly? Yes. Sticky? Very.
Block C · Travel Verbs
El autobús llega a las dos.
The bus arrives at two.
Tip Llega: the double-ll sounds like a soft English "y" — "ye-GA". Practice aloud.
Block D · Prepositions
De Madrid a Barcelona en tren.
From Madrid to Barcelona by train.
Tip de = from · a = to · en = by (mode). One rule, three words.
Block H · Problems
Estoy perdido / perdida.
I am lost.
Tip Match your gender: male = perdido, female = perdida. Only the last vowel changes.
Block B · Key Places
Tome el metro hasta la parada Aeropuerto.
Take the metro to the Airport stop.
Tip Aeropuerto = aero (air) + puerto (port) = airport. Same logic as English "air-port."
Block G · Buying a Ticket
Un billete para Toledo, por favor. ¿Cuánto cuesta?
A ticket to Toledo, please. How much does it cost?
Tip Lead with "para" (purpose) not "a" when buying. Cuesta ← costar = to cost. Near-twin of English "cost."
🧭 Preposition Map
en
Mode of transport — how you travel. Voy en tren / en avión / en taxi. Exception: a pie (on foot — no vehicle).
a
Destination — where you're going. El tren de Madrid a Barcelona.
de
Origin — where it starts. El autobús de Sevilla a Málaga.
🔤 Gender: perdido / perdida
Masculine
perdido
male speaker
Feminine
perdida

Use estoy (not soy) — temporary state. Being lost is temporary.
💡 Power Tips
billete (Spain) vs boleto (Latin America) — both mean ticket. Diana = Spain → use billete.
¿A qué hora…? always starts with a, never en. Resist the reflex.
Hay un retraso — "HAY rhymes with 'hey, we're late!'" Drill as one chunk.
Autobús stress: au-to-BÚS. The accent mark is your stress guide.
Andén → an-DÉN. Without the written accent you'd flatten it and sound unsure.

Context Dialogues

🚌 At the Bus Stop
A Perdona, ¿a qué hora sale el autobús para Córdoba?
Excuse me, what time does the bus to Córdoba leave?
B Sale a las tres y cuarto.
It leaves at quarter past three.
A ¿Cuánto tarda?
How long does it take?
B Unas dos horas.
About two hours.
🚆 At the Train Station
A Buenos días. Un billete para Sevilla, por favor.
Good morning. A ticket to Seville, please.
B ¿Ida o ida y vuelta?
One-way or round-trip?
A Ida y vuelta. ¿Cuánto cuesta?
Round-trip. How much does it cost?
B Cuarenta euros.
Forty euros.
✈️ At the Airport
A Disculpe, ¿dónde está el aeropuerto de Málaga?
Excuse me, where is Málaga airport?
B Tome el metro hasta la parada Aeropuerto.
Take the metro to the Airport stop.
A ¿A qué hora llega el próximo metro?
What time does the next metro arrive?
B Llega en diez minutos.
It arrives in ten minutes.
⚠️ Problem Scenario
A Perdona, estoy perdido. ¿Hay un retraso?
Excuse me, I'm lost. Is there a delay?
B Sí, el tren llega con veinte minutos de retraso.
Yes, the train arrives 20 minutes late.
A ¿En qué andén?
Which platform?
B En el andén dos.
Platform two.
