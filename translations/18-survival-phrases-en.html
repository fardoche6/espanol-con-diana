Emergency Shouts Block F

🚨
¡Socorro!
Help! (classic, shout loudly)
🆘
¡Ayuda!
Help! (also common)
Urgent
¡Llamen a la policía!
Call the police!
Llamen = command to a crowd (plural). One person: ¡Llame! (formal) / ¡Llama! (informal)
Urgent
¡Llamen a una ambulancia!
Call an ambulance!
Same plural imperative. Use when you need medical help fast.
Urgent
Estoy perdido / perdida.
I'm lost. (masc. / fem.)
Match your gender: -o if you identify masc., -a if fem.
Regional
¡Auxilio!
Help! (Mexico, Central America)
Less common but understood everywhere. Third emergency shout to know.

I Don't Understand Block A

Both
No entiendo.
I don't understand.
Stress: no en-TIEN-do. Most common in Latin America.
Formal
No comprendo.
I don't understand.
Slightly more formal / written register. Both correct everywhere.
Chain
No entiendo nada.
I don't understand anything.
Adds urgency. Use when completely lost.

Please Repeat Block B

Formal
¿Puede repetir, por favor?
Can you repeat, please?
Rising intonation at end. Use with strangers, officials, shop staff.
Informal
Repite, por favor.
Repeat, please.
Tuteo (tú form). Use with peers, young people, friends.

Speak Slower Block C

+ Gesture
Más despacio, por favor.
More slowly, please.
Gesture: flat palm pressing slowly downward. Use this every time.
Formal
Hable más despacio.
Speak more slowly. (usted)
-e ending = usted (formal command)
Informal
Habla más despacio.
Speak more slowly. (tú)
-a ending = tú (informal command). Also: más lento (Caribbean variant)

What Does This Mean? Block D

¿Qué significa...?
What does ... mean?
Use for a word you see or hear. Point + say ¿Qué significa esto?
¿Cómo se dice... en español?
How do you say ... in Spanish?
Use for a word in your head (EN→ES direction). Fill in the blank.

Can You Help Me? Block E

Formal
¿Me puede ayudar?
Can you help me?
Me comes first — indirect object before the verb in Spanish.
Informal
¿Puedes ayudarme?
Can you help me?
Pronoun attached to infinitive instead. Same meaning.
Quick
Ayuda, por favor.
Help, please.
Ayuda alone = noun used as mild call. Always add por favor.

Show Me / Write It Down Block I

¿Puede escribirlo?
Can you write it down?
escribirlo = write + it (lo). Lifesaver when audio is unclear.
Formal
Muéstreme.
Show me.
Point at a map, sign, or menu while saying this.
Informal
Muéstrame.
Show me.
Only the vowel changes: Muéstr-e-me → Muéstr-a-me

I Don't Speak Well Block G

Hablo poco español.
I speak a little Spanish.
Strategic opener — triggers slower, simpler replies from natives.
Soy principiante.
I'm a beginner.
Pronunciation: prin-see-PYAN-te. Invites patience.

Perdón vs Disculpe vs Lo siento Block H + K

Perdón
Sorry / Excuse me
Casual. Two uses: (1) apology after bumping into someone — falling tone; (2) get someone's attention — rising tone.
"Perdón, ¿me puede ayudar?"
Disculpe
Excuse me (formal)
Formal attention-getter. Use in shops, offices, restaurants. NEVER use to express remorse.
"Disculpe, señora."
Lo siento
I'm truly sorry
Emotional apology for real mistakes or harm. NOT used to get attention. Condolences, serious errors.
"Lo siento mucho."

Regional Variations
Latin America: entender dominates over comprender in everyday speech.  |  Mexico / Central America: ¡Auxilio! is a third emergency shout.  |  Caribbean: más lento used instead of más despacio.  |  Río de la Plata (Argentina/Uruguay): no entendí (past tense) softens politely.  |  Spain: perdona / perdone (verb forms) common alongside the noun perdón.
Tips & Tricks
  • Chain phrases for maximum clarity: Perdón — hablo poco español. ¿Puede repetir más despacio?
  • ¡Socorro! carries better in noisy crowds than ¡Ayuda! — the hard consonants cut through noise.
  • Always pair Muéstreme / Muéstrame with a pointing gesture — removes all ambiguity.
  • Lo siento is NEVER used to get attention — that mistake sounds very strange to native ears.
  • Rising tone on Perdón = excuse me (calling waiter); falling tone = I'm sorry (bumped someone).
