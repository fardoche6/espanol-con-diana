Tips & Tricks

1
-o boy, -a girl: the adjective ending matches the noun's grammatical gender, not a person's gender.
2
Invariable shortcut: if the dictionary form ends in -e or a consonant, it almost certainly doesn't change for gender.
3
pelirrojo mnemonic: pelo (hair) + rojo (red) = red-haired. Compound words reveal their own meaning.
4
Ser for descriptions: always use es / son for permanent physical traits. "Ella es alta" — never está alta.
5
marrón trap: no -a ending (invariable), but plural adds -es and drops the accent: marrones.
6
Adjective after noun: Spanish defaults to noun-first order — "el coche rojo," not "el rojo coche" at A1.
