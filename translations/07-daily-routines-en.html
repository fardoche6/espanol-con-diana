Morning · La Mañana
☀️

Me levanto a las siete.

levantarse · reflexive Reflexive Verb

I get up at seven o'clock.

The me anchors the action to yourself — you lift yourself up.

🚿

Me ducho y me visto.

ducharse · vestirse · reflexive pair Reflexive Chain

I shower and I get dressed.

Both actions return to you — me goes on each verb in a chain.

☕

Desayuno y tomo el café.

desayunar · tomar · no reflexive Regular -ar Verbs

I have breakfast and drink coffee.

No me here — the action goes toward the food, not yourself.

Work & Study · El Trabajo
🏃

Salgo de casa a las ocho.

salir · irregular yo form Irregular yo

I leave the house at eight.

Salgo is irregular only in yo — all other forms follow the pattern.

💼

Empiezo a trabajar a las nueve.

empezar · e→ie stem change Stem-changing

I start working at nine o'clock.

Empezar + a + infinitive — never skip the a.

Midday · El Mediodía
🍽️

Como a las dos de la tarde.

comer · regular -er verb Cultural Note

I have lunch at two in the afternoon.

Spain's main meal is at 2 pm — much later than in English-speaking countries.

🏠

Vuelvo a casa a las seis.

volver · o→ue stem change Stem-changing

I return home at six o'clock.

Vuelvo → vuelves → vuelve but volvemos stays regular.

Evening & Night · La Noche
🌙

Ceno a las nueve de la noche.

cenar · regular -ar verb Cultural Note

I have dinner at nine at night.

9–10 pm dinner is completely normal in Spain. Don't be surprised!

📖

Me relajo y leo un poco.

relajarse (reflexive) · leer (not reflexive) Mixed Pair

I relax and I read a little.

Relajarse is reflexive; leer is not — learn the contrast.

😴

Me acuesto y me duermo.

acostarse · dormirse · both o→ue Reflexive + Stem

I go to bed and I fall asleep.

Me duermo = I fall asleep (onset). Duermo alone = I sleep (state).

Reflexive Trap

Remove me/te/se — does the meaning survive? Ducho al perro (I bathe the dog) vs. me ducho (I bathe myself). If the action returns to you, you need the pronoun.

Clock vs. Period

Use a las ocho for clock precision. Use por la mañana for fuzzy period. Never swap them: "I work in the morning" ≠ "I work at the morning."

Double Negative

Spanish welcomes double negatives. No como nunca tarde and Nunca como tarde are both correct. Stop translating English grammar rules directly.
