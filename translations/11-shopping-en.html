Shop Window — Key Phrases at a Glance

Core Phrases — Annotated

Greetings

Buenos días.

Good morning.

BWE-nos DEE-as

Use until noon. Switch to Buenas tardes from 12 pm onwards. When uncertain, Buenas alone works at any hour.

Shopkeeper → You

¿En qué puedo ayudarle?

How can I help you?

en ke PWE-do a-yu-DAR-le

Formal usted form. If they say ayudarte (casual), don't be confused — both mean the same.

Browsing

Estoy mirando, gracias.

Just looking, thanks.

es-TOY mi-RAN-do, GRA-syas

The single most useful phrase before a trip. Polite; ends further questions gracefully.

Requesting — Polite

Quería una camisa azul.

I'd like a blue shirt.

ke-RI-a OO-na ka-MI-sa a-SOUL

Quería (imperfect) is the polite Spanish equivalent of "I would like." Softer than Quiero.

Requesting — Checking stock

¿Tiene talla M?

Do you have size M?

TYE-ne TAL-ya EME

For shoes, swap talla → número: ¿Tiene número 42?

Searching

Busco una bolsa grande.

I'm looking for a large bag.

BUS-ko OO-na BOL-sa GRAN-de

More open than ¿Tiene? — ideal when you're not sure they stock it.

Price

¿Cuánto cuesta?

How much does it cost?

KWAN-to KWES-ta

Multiple items → ¿Cuánto cuestan? Prices are spoken with con: €5.50 = cinco euros con cincuenta.

Negotiating

¿Tiene algo más barato?

Do you have anything cheaper?

TYE-ne AL-go mas ba-RA-to

Works in fixed-price shops. At open markets, follow up with ¿Me hace un precio? to open negotiation.

Closing the sale

Me lo llevo. Pago en efectivo.

I'll take it. I'll pay in cash.

me lo YE-vo. PA-go en e-fek-TI-vo

Me lo llevo (m.) / Me la llevo (f.) — the pronoun matches the item's gender.

Tips & Tricks

Cultural notes for confident shopping

★

Always greet first

In Spain, entering a small shop without a greeting is considered rude. Open with Buenos días or Buenas before saying anything else.

¥

Haggling etiquette

Fixed-price shops (Spain, most cities) don't bargain. Open markets welcome ¿Me hace un precio? In Mexico, saying está muy caro with a smile often opens a counter-offer.

G

Gender agreement

Colors ending in -o/-a agree: zapato rojo / camisa roja. Colors ending in -e or a consonant don't change: zapato verde / camisa verde.

S

Sizes across regions

Spain uses EU sizes (38/40/42 for clothes). S/M/L/XL are universally understood. For shoes ask ¿Qué número? — not talla.
