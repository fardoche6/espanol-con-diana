Farewells
final goodbye

Literally: "To God" (archaic)
Adiós

🙌

Hasta luego

Literally: "Until tomorrow"

Name · Origin · Age · Profession
name · informal
Me llamo [Nombre]
🪪
🇺🇸My name is [Name]
Literally: "I call myself [Name]"
Me llamo
⚠ ll = Y sound: "me YA-mo", not "me LA-mo"
question · asking name
¿Cómo te llamas?
❓
🇺🇸What's your name?
Literally: "How do you call yourself?"
Cómo te llamas
✦ Accent on Có- marks the stress AND the question.
origin
Soy de [Lugar]
📍
🇺🇸I'm from [Place]
Literally: "I am from [Place]"
✦ Permanent fact → uses ser (soy), not estar.
⚠ ¿De dónde eres? = Where are you from?
age · the big trap
Tengo [X] años
🎂
🇺🇸I am [X] years old
Literally: "I have [X] years"
Tengo años
⚠ NEVER "Soy 30 años" — age uses tener, not ser!
profession
Soy [profesión]
💼
🇺🇸I am a [profession]
Literally: "I am [profession]" — no article!
estudiante médico/a profesor/a ingeniero/a artista
⚠ No "un/una" before profession: Soy médico ✓, not Soy un médico ✗
Social Glue — Politeness & Pleased-to-Meet
first meeting
Mucho gusto
🤝
🇺🇸Pleased to meet you
Literally: "Much pleasure"
✦ Cognate: gusto → "gusto" in English. Easy!
reply to mucho gusto
Igualmente
🇺🇸Likewise / Same to you
Literally: "Equally"
✦ One word. Saves you repeating mucho gusto.
delighted to meet you
Encantado / Encantada
🇺🇸Delighted to meet you
Literally: "Enchanted" — cognate!
⚠ Ending = SPEAKER's gender: male → -o, female → -a
please
Por favor
🙏
🇺🇸Please
Literally: "For a favour"
✦ Goes at the END: Un café, por favor.
thank you · reply
Gracias / De nada
💚
🇺🇸Thank you / You're welcome
De nada = "Of nothing"
✦ De nada ≈ French "de rien". Same pattern.
sorry · excuse me
Perdón
🙇
🇺🇸Sorry / Excuse me
Literally: "Pardon" — cognate!
✦ Two uses: apology + getting attention.
Disculpe = slightly more formal for attention.
Common Traps — Don't Say These
Soy 30 años
Tengo 30 años
Age uses tener (to have), not ser (to be). You "have" years in Spanish.
Soy bien
Estoy bien
States use estar. Being well is temporary — use estoy, not soy.
Soy un médico
Soy médico
No article before profession after ser in Spanish.
di-as (flat)
dí·as (2 syllables)
The written accent on días marks the break — say dee·as, not dee-ya.
me LA-mo
me YA-mo
ll sounds like the English Y in "yes" — never like the letter L.
man-yana
mah-NYA-na
The ñ is a distinct letter. It adds the "ny" sound — like "canyon".
