Telling Time
Hours on the clock
Singular for 1:00, plural for all others.
Es la unaes la OO-nah
It's one o'clock
Son las dosson las doss
It's two o'clock
Son las tres y media... ee MEH-dyah
It's three thirty
Son las nueve y cuarto... ee KWAHR-toh
It's nine fifteen
Son las cinco menos diez... MEH-nos dyess
It's ten to five (4:50)
Rule: Only 1:00 uses "Es la una". Every other hour: "Son las ___". Mixing singular/plural is the #1 A1 time error.

Dates & Appointments
Full date sentences
Formula: el + day + de + month (+ a las + time)
el quince de mayoel KIN-seh deh MY-oh
the fifteenth of May
el primero de eneroel pree-MEH-ro
the first of January
¿Cuándo es tu cumpleaños?KWAN-doh ess too
When is your birthday?
La cita es el martes a las tresla SEE-tah
The appointment is Tuesday at three
Hoy es lunes, el dos de junioOY ess LOO-ness
Today is Monday, June 2nd
Day 1 only: "el primero" — never "el uno". For all other days use cardinal numbers.

Decimal separator: Spanish uses a comma (€3,50), not a period. "Con" links euros and cents — it's mandatory in formal speech.
Full Sentences
Appointment & event language
Combining date + day + time in one sentence.
El concierto es el viernes a las ocho de la noche
The concert is Friday at eight at night
¿En qué mes estamos?en keh mess ess-TAH-moss
What month are we in?
¿Qué día es hoy?keh DEE-ah ess OY
What day is today?
Mi cumpleaños es el tres de marzomee koom-pleh-AHN-yos
My birthday is March 3rd
Date format: day / month / year (never US month-first)
